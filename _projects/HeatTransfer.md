I chose to analyze a question from our second homework assignment, as I felt it was representitive of the skills I have learned in the class so far. I started by doing an energy balance, which is one of the key concepts from the class, to balance energy added in the system with energy leaving. The energy leaving the system is a function of surface area, as well as the insulation properties. I then used the energy balance to find the power required to keep a water heater at 50 Celcius with and without insulation. This is done by calculating the heat drop across the wall of the tank, and using that outer temperature as the inner temperature for the insulation. Finally, you can balance the power into the tank with the ambient temperature of the surroundings to find the answer.

The reason I chose this question is that I feel the way I went about learning the crucial skills set me up for the rest of the course. By looking at course notes, reading the notes, and researching on the internet I was able to find the equations and skills I needed to complete the question. This has been a consistent theme across the problem sets in Heat Transfer, and these research skills have allowed me to teach myself how to solve difficult questions
