For my Mechatronics class my group was tasked with designing a robot capable of navigating a board and collecting small wooden blocks. Our robot was then pitted against others in one minute competitions to see which one could gather more in the alloted time. Ultimitely, we won 8 of our 9 matches and placed in the top eight of the 50+ groups entered.

What made this competion especially challenging was our limited resources. We were only allowed to use an Arduino Uno with no libraries, and had a budget of only $40 to create a functioning robot capable of smartly navigating the playspace and contending with competitor's strategies. My group's design ethos focused on creating a consistent and robust robot that could hold onto blocks dispite collisions with other bots, as well as using QTI sensors in comparator circuits for ultra-reliable border detection. This enabled us to spend the full minute collecting blocks after others had failed, and produced a result we are extremly proud of.
